AUDIO-VISUAL ART EVENTS IN VR

This is an art and research project that was carried out during the lockdown periods of the pandemic. Twelve virtual audio-visual art events were organised. I designed the scenes and produced the visual artefacts including 3D models, videos and stills. The final scenes and assets were put together on Mozilla Spoke and the experiences were hosted on Mozilla Hubs, an accessible and open-source VR platform, which the guests could access via web browsers or HMDs.

In this event series that took place in 2021, I hosted an electronic music artist at each event and they were asked to "imagine a world where we are not able to dance again, what would that sound like?. The aim of this project was to gain insights on how participants interact with both the VR environment and each other in the context of virtual audio-visual art events; and whether this environment can be a better alternative to live or recorded online streams.

It was observed that the guests were highly engaged in these events. Their feedback included:

"More realistic and engaging experience compared to online streams"

"Being able to navigate in the space freely with their avatars"

"Experiencing spatial/3D audio like in the physical reality"

"Interacting with others using real-time audio, video and chat-box features"
